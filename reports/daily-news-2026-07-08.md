[Cameron Emanuel-Burns,](/author/cameron-emanuel-burns) Reporter, FinTech Futures July 7, 2026 schedule 2 Min Read The [UK's Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)](/regulations-compliance/fca-pushes-stablecoin-innovation-in-2026) has published a new review examining the impact of AI on retail financial services, revealing existing consumer appetite for agentic AI. The [Mills Review](https://www.fca.org.uk/publication/corporate/the-mills-review.pdf) , led by FCA executive director Sheldon Mills, blended feedback from consumer groups, firms, international stakeholders, trade bodies, and technology providers. The research incorporated a survey of over 5,000 UK financial services consumers, focus groups, and a comparative analysis of AI implementation approaches in other countries. Arguing that "AI could reshape the sector by 2030", the paper identifies a shift in the retail financial sector from "human-led, towards AI-enabled, continuous and delegated services". The research indicates that one in five UK adults are already receptive to AI-driven decision-making, though it warns that consumer adoption will depend on "trust, control and access". Concerns persist regarding how the technology could "amplify risks associated with fraud, cyber security and consumer harm". Related: [CaixaBank completes first agentic payment with Visa](/ai-in-fintech/caixabank-ai-agent-payment-visa) Demand for AI as a primary decision-maker is strongest in areas "where choices feel complex or high-stakes, particularly debt advice, pensions and investments", according to the review. Around 26% of consumers surveyed say that they trust general-purpose AI tools for financial advice. Additionally, the paper states that [agentic AI](/ai-in-fintech/trust-is-a-new-currency-but-can-we-trust-ai-agents) can address "longstanding weaknesses in retail financial markets". It suggests the technology could help bridge the advice gap, given that only 9% of consumers currently use traditional advice services, while also tackling issues such as suboptimal saving behaviours, the protection gap, and financial exclusion, with approximately 900,000 individuals underbanked in the country. The review concludes that the technology can "help consumers make better decisions, access more suitable products and manage their finances more effectively". The paper presents seven priority recommendations to the regulator: securing and adapting the "regulatory perimeter", bolstering system-wide coordination and oversight, monitoring the shift to autonomous models while adapting regulatory frameworks, establishing foundations for agentic finance, implementing an AI-enabled agentic supervisory model, creating a public-interest AI-driven financial capability service, and scaling the FCA's AI Lab to advance safe AI adoption. The AI Lab initiative, [introduced in late 2024](/ai-in-fintech/fca-debuts-ai-lab-to-promote-safe-use-of-ai-in-uk-financial-sector) , has expanded to include programmes such as AI Input Zone, AI Spotlight, AI Sprint, and the [Supercharged Sandbox](/ai-in-fintech/uks-fca-to-launch-ai-sandbox-in-collaboration-with-nvidia) , which debuted last October with Nvidia's support.
