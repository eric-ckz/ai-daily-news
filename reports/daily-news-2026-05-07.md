   - 摘要：* [Fibre](/fixed-networks/fibre) * [AI](/digital-ecosystem/ai) * [Telecoms Infrastructure](/telecoms-infrastructure) * [News](/latest-news) # Nvidia buys into optical with Corning investment US chip giant Nvidia has announced a significant investment in glass maker Corning, which will dramatically increase production capacity as a consequence. [Scott Bicheno,](/author/scott-bicheno) Editorial Director May 6, 2026 2 Min Read source: nvidia As the biggest single beneficiary of the current AI boom, Nvidia has all the incentive it needs to stoke and extend it. In the latest example of it throwing money at AI-related companies, Nvidia is investing in glass maker Corning, which is responsible for much of the fibre used in connectivity, as well as other optical materials and the glass on your smartphone screen. The deal is being positioned as a long-term partnership, with the inevitable nods to the current US political agenda of boosting domestic manufacturing. As a result of the partnership, Corning has committed to building three new manufacturing plants that will increase its fibre production capacity by 50% and optical connectivity manufacturing capacity by ten times.  It’s thought that this signifies a further Nvidia commitment to co-packaged optics, through which fibre replaces copper in the interconnections between chips in order to increase bandwidth and reduce energy consumption. Here’s a Corning [explanation](https://www.corning.com/optical-communications/emea/en/home/the-signal-network-blog/what-is-co-packaged-optics.html) of the concept. And, of course, Nvidia also has an interest in regular uses of fibre for data center connectivity. Related: [Lumen goes global with $475m Alkira deal](/fibre/lumen-goes-global-with-475m-alkira-deal) “AI is driving the largest infrastructure buildout of our time — and a once-in-a-generation opportunity to reinvigorate American manufacturing and supply chains,” said Jensen Huang, CEO of Nvidia. “Together with Corning, we are inventing the future of computing with advanced optical technologies — building the foundation for AI infrastructure where intelligence moves at the speed of light while advancing the proud tradition of Made in America.” “This partnership is proof that AI is not just a technology story, it is a manufacturing story, and it is happening here in the United States,” said Wendell Weeks, CEO of Corning. “Together with Nvidia, we are ensuring the critical technologies powering AI are invented, engineered and built in America.” Yeah, we get the message lads. Financial terms weren’t revealed in the press release but a Corning SEC filing detailed a ‘pre-funded warrant’ of $500 million worth of Corning shares, together with the option for Nvidia to buy up to 15 million more at a slight price premium of $180 each.
