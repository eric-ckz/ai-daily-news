   - 摘要：* [Vulnerabilities & Threats](/vulnerabilities-threats) # The Attribution Gap: Why IAM Fails the Superhuman Identity AI-driven identities are reshaping identity access management (IAM), exposing gaps in security, intelligence, and accountability. April 27, 2026 4 Min Read SOURCE: BlackJack3D / Getty Images Sponsored by JumpCloud Identity used to be simple: a person logged in, performed an action, and left a traceable footprint. Accountability was direct and attribution was clear. IAM systems were built around this one-to-one model. That assumption is breaking quickly because actions are no longer performed solely by humans but increasingly by autonomous AI agents operating on their behalf, often at scale, across systems, and without direct human initiation.  Modern environments now include three identity types: human, non-human, and agentic. Agentic identities are AI-driven entities that act on behalf of users, systems, and workflows. They operate at machine speed, make autonomous decisions, and often inherit human-level privileges without human oversight. As a result, security teams face an attribution gap and can no longer reliably answer who performed a given action.  Agentic identities are not simple scripts or traditional service accounts.
