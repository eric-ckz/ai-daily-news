[Gagandeep Kaur,](/author/gagandeep-kaur)Contributing Editor April 15, 2026 3 Min Read (Source: Nokia) Nokia and Blaize, an AI chip designer company, are moving beyond early-stage collaboration to address what they believe is a major bottleneck in the growing AI ecosystem: scalable but cost-efficient inference. Following their initial Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) earlier this year, the two companies are now developing a joint reference architecture to simplify hybrid AI deployments across edge, cloud and data center environments in the Asia-Pacific (APAC) region. The partnership seeks to address the three challenges that enterprises and service providers currently struggle with, including ensuring end-to-end performance, deployment complexity and reducing the cost of AI infrastructure. The two companies would be using the Nokia Innovation Lab in Singapore to develop, test, validate and deploy AI solutions for the customers. "Customers today are struggling to stitch together AI silicon, software frameworks, networking, security and lifecycle management. This joint partnership allows us to provide a pre-integrated validated stack with AI, hardware, compute, hardware inference, networking and also the automation to work together," said Dion Leung, Head of AI and Cloud – APAC at Nokia. "AI workloads at the edge often cannot be justified by GPU economics or power budget alone. Together, we can provide an energy-efficient AI platform and it also allows us to place the workload optimally across the far edge or near edge, helping us to reduce both CAPEX and OPEX," added Leung. The move comes at a time when demand is shifting from model training towards inference as organizations are keen to monetize their AI investments. "What we are seeing now is a clear shift in demand from training to inference with cloud providers reporting growth of 15% to 50% in inference-related workloads as customers look to move from experimentation to real-world deployment. The organizations are asking how to actually deploy applications and generate value from their AI investments," said Joseph Sulistyo, Senior VP of Global Marketing at Blaize.  This is leading to focus on outcome-driven solutions that can quickly move from pilot to production, according to Sulistyo, and it is this gap that the Nokia-Blaize collaboration wants to address. While developed APAC economies such as Singapore, Japan and Australia are advancing toward cloud-native and edge-based architectures, fast-growing markets like India, Indonesia and Vietnam are still experimenting with AI deployment models and monetization strategies. Nokia and Blaize believe that the next two to three years will see the market move from experimentation to the development of domain specific outcome-driven solutions.   Nokia's partnership with Blaize in APAC complements its deal with chip giant Nvidia last year. [Nvidia acquired a 2.9% stake in Nokia](/5g/nvidia-takes-1b-stake-in-nokia-which-promises-5g-and-6g-overhaul) by investing $1 billion in the company to accelerate the development of AI-RAN (Radio Access Network) in October 2025. Nokia and Blaize face strong competition in the region from several players, including Nvidia and Qualcomm on the compute side, as well as Huawei and Ericsson, which has a partnership with Intel.
